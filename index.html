Joy of Synths is a non-musicians guide to creating sounds with analog synthesizers for your videos, podcasts, and sampling for music.

What is a Synth?

Synth is short for synthesizer, an electronic music instrument that synthesizes sound with voltage.

Think grade-school photosynthesis, and how plants convert light energy into oxygen. Sound synthesis converts electrical energy into tones.

Note for Note

These tones can be unpitched like a drum or pitched to raise the sound from low to high notes. However, please accept that an analog synthesizer probably will NOT match a piano scale. If it does, it may drift away from it. This is also called tracking and it frustrates musicians expecting a tuned instrument. This inconsistency is what's so suprising about synthesizers. Its also why recording everything is recommended. Joy of Synths focuses on single sound events for non-musicians.

Getting Started with Synthesizers

Getting to sound from silence can be frustrating. My first efforts in 2017 with physical synthesizers was getting it produce anything. In Spring 2019, Yesenia introduced me to the littleBits Korg Synth Kit.

My shopping mania latched onto buying pretty much every retail kit.

Research, Research, Research

Sometimes it helps to start at the start. I traveled to MIT and Harvard libraries for Electronic Music books. My last research trip was to MIAMI, where all my confusion coalesced to a simple reality.

Voltage signals

A synth starts with voltage to generate signals that travel through modules converts them into sound signals. Joy of Synths will walk you through creating sounds with abbreviations like CV, OSC, MOD, EG, VCA, VCO, Pitch, and more. These make sense in practice.
